⎈Some of my thoughts about Red Hat <<The Open Organization>> 2018-03-23

The benefit of Open Source and Open Organization

The most significant misunderstanding of open source is “Tom Sawyer” model[1] of collaborations: you stare at your own pieces of “cake”, and wish people could jump in and help you instead of helping each other to make the whole “cake” bigger so everyone could be benefit from it. Red Hat is the latter one: to be the catalyst in communities of customers, contributors, and partners creating better technology the open source way.

Being catalyst is an insightful thinking which need truly collaborations from different organizations who willing to give up their own “shrewdness” and work together. To be honest, I don’t know what’s the future of open source will be like, just like I don’t know when human could colonize Mars, but I firmly believe we will be there someday.

There may have an inaccurate metaphor in my humble opinion to support my belief, the open source way qualified sharing economy: connection is more important than ownership, collaborations make resource being shared to make the society more efficient. Think about Airbnb, Uber, Google, Facebook… they don’t own contents/properties, but they have the connections. Sharing software is build a community provide an open space/connections for people to share intellectual properties, ideas, contributions from different countries, corporations. It will definitely lower the cost of software development cost, making the products more creativity, make hard problems being solved, is about making the world more efficient, making the “cake” bigger in a holistic view.

Top Down vs Bottom Up

Top Down hierarchy works for traditional company which the environment and goal is stable enough and everyone need to do is just follow the “correct” order do it efficiently, But Bottom up hierarchy works for company need creativity, fast move which means the environment and goal cannot be easily foreseen.

This make me remind an example of the Chinese Civil War[2], The Communist Party of China(CPC) win the war against Kuomintang-led government(KMT), the reason could be explained by “Top Down vs Bottom Up” theory, KMT uses Top Down hierarchy which seems more efficiency, but it could be had a severe problem during the war when the communication in the hierarchy being blocked. But Bottom Up hierarchy is make sure everyone even poor peasants and workers understand what they fight for and why (farmland reform) and have the inner momentum to fight fiercely even there’s no one giving them order. (igniting their passion)

Creativity

Previously, my understanding of creativity is narrowed: I always thought creativity is only some scientists pops up some brilliant ideas, or win some new fundamental finding prize in science, or some big corporation invent some new intellectual patents. But I was wrong, actually creativity is everywhere, think about apple, each release of the iPhone, the new product blows everybody’s mind, and then everyone starts to follow, turn out being the number one of the industry need creativity. So as Red Hat, we being the number one company of open source industry, everything we do will impact the open source community, and if we being creative enough, that open source could have the chance to be more glorious.

Freedom, Openness, Diversity

Freedom, Openness and Diversity is the catalyst of creativity. Imagine what if scientific research world would be like without freedom, openness and diversity: political interference will lead to scientific fraud, benefit-oriented interference will lead to we miss some potential evolutionary inventions. Think about MIT media labs which was originally formed by some marginalized but talented professors whose research is not that “beneficial enough”, and the MIT give them a chance to form their own lab and boom: touchscreen, electronic ink, scratch programming design for kids, artificial prosthesis, wearable device, GPS vehicle tracking system etc all comes from media lab which are incredible inventions. Imagine each countries’ scientist/engineer all incarcerated in their own countries, never had the chance to have an open discussion/conference. As second industrial revolution passed almost a hundred year, the scientific/engineering problems we are tackling are way more difficult and complicated than anything before, so having a freedom, openness, diversity environment to collaborations are important. And I have to admit, for this area, US as an ethnic melt pot is way more advanced than any other countries.
